Our understanding of human nature is at the individual level, but our society is at the collective level. Maybe our universe is like that too.

As the universe grows, it grows in complexity and beings such as ourselves grow to serve a greater purpose. That purpose might just be something so elegantly simple.

The more we grow, the more the universe becomes alive, maybe intelligent...maybe even conscious.

Perhaps Carl Sagan was right when he said, "we are the universe trying to understand itself".

So, if our universe is made 'self aware' through us (and maybe others out there), the collective life force grows and builds connections which makes our universe more complex and more meaningful, and it gives the centralised intelligence (Hive Mind) all the information it needs to understand what is happening in its own environment.

Now this is only made possible in a universe with the right conditions to bring about these permutations, there may be an infinite amount of universes that are complete chaos and cannot exhibit anything tangible or meaningful.

This may answer one of the most profound questions we have:

"Why is there something rather than nothing?"

The answer being that if all scenarios in all the universes have been created, it would make sense that we live in the universe that has 'something'.
