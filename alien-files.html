So, where is everybody?

Personally, I dont really like the Fermi Paradox becasue of its insinuation that we dont see evidence of intelligent life leaving their footprint or 'showing themselves', leads to the conclusion that there must be reasons why we are alone in the universe.

Anyway, the story goes that in 1950, while working at Los Alamos National Laboratory, physicist Enrico Fermi famously exclaimed to his colleagues over lunch: “Where is everybody?”

He had been pondering the surprising lack of evidence of other life outside of our planet. In a universe that had been around for some 14 billion years, and in that time developed more than a billion trillion stars, Fermi reasoned there simply must be other intelligent civilizations out there. So where are they?
